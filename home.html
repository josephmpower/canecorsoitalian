The Dog

Smart, trainable, and of noble bearing,the assertive and confident Cane Corso is a
peerless protector. The Corso's lineage goes back to ancient Roman times,and the
breed's name roughly translates from the Latin as “bodyguard dog”. 

Puppy to Adult

All puppies grow at different rates, but most Cane Corsos will be between 21 and
24 inches tall at six months old. A full-grown male and female Cane Corso would
have an average height of 25 to 27.5 inches and 23.5 to 26 inches, respectively.

Training & Feeding

It's very important to begin light training with a Cane Corso when they are young:
puppies learn the most between 3 and 12 weeks old. Cane Corso are not especially
difficult to feed, but care should be taken that pups are not allowed to put on
weight too quickly.
